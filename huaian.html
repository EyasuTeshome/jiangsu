Ming Ancestors Mausoleum

The Ming Ancestors Mausoleum is the first imperial mausoleum complex of the Ming dynasty, and a cenotaph located north of the former Sizhou City, Yangjiadun, in present-day Xuyi County, Huaian City, by Hongze Lake, north of Huai River Jiangsu Province, China. It was built by Zhu Yuanzhang, the founding emperor of the Ming dynasty, in 1385, for his great-great-grandfather (posthumously styled Emperor Xuan), great-grandfather (posthumously styled Emperor Heng) and grandfather (posthumously styled Emperor Yu). It is also the actual burial site of Zhu Chuyi, Zhu Yuanzhang's grandfather

Zhou Enlai's Hometown

This tourist attraction is made up of Zhou Enlai Memorial Hall, Former Residence of Zhou Enlai, the old site of Zhou Enlai's school in his childhood, Consort Prince Lane and Hexia Ancient Town. Zhou Enlai Memorial Hall is located in Tao Hua Yin outside the north gate of ancient Huaian. It is made up of a memorial island, broad waters and ring green coverage around the lake, covering a total area of 350,000 square meters, of which 70% are waters, the total construction area is 21,166 square meters, and the total exhibition area is 7,880 square meters.
