Get to Know Nanjing

Enjoy Your Time

Nanjing is the capital of Jiangsu province of the People's Republic of China and the second largest city in the East China region, with 11 districts, an administrative area of 6,600 km (2,500 sq mi) and a total population of 8,505,500 as of 2019 .Situated in the Yangtze River Delta region, Nanjing has a prominent place in Chinese history and culture, having served as the capital of various Chinese dynasties, kingdoms and republican governments dating from the 3rd century to 1949, and has thus long been a major center of culture, education, research, politics, economy, transport networks and tourism, being the home to one of the world's largest inland ports.

Presidential Palace of Nanjing

A place with a long history and prominent history, the Presidential Palace of Nanjing was the Weaving department of Jiangning and the Administrative headquarters of Jiangnan during the Qing Dynasty. Sun Yat-sen who was appointed interim president of Nanjing on 1st January 1912 used this place as the president’s office and meeting room. The place also served as the presidential residence of the Kuomintang government. The building has a mixture of Chinese and Western architectural styles. It is generously proportioned and intriguing to look at.

Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum

This is the burial place of Dr. Sun Yat-sen, the father of the Warlord Era. It is located at Purple Mountain in the east suburb of Nanjing City, Jiangsu Province, covering an area of 80, 000 square meters. It is considered the holy land of Chinese people locally and abroad. Dr. Sun Yat-sen (1866-1925) was a great revolutionary that led the Chinese people to overthrow the corrupt rule of Qing dynasty (1644-1911) and ended 2000 years of the feudal monarchy system, bringing China into a new age. The mausoleum is a must see when you are in Nanjing because of its great historical significance, grand architecture, and beautiful scenery.

Confucius Temple

The temple was originally built in the Song Dynasty to commemorate Confucius. It is located beside Gongyuan Street on the north bank of Qinhuai River. However the temple was destroyed and rebuilt several times.The current temple was rebuilt in 1987 after it was destroyed by Japanese Aggressors during World War 2. At present moment the temple is the main scenic spot of the Qinhuai River Scenery. It also includes the China Imperial Examination Examination Museum and Xue Gong (The Imperial Academy). A majestic 6 meter high huge portrait of Confucius and 38 mural paintings reflecting the life story of Confucius is something worth to admire when you visit this place.

Nanjing City Wall

The City Wall of Nanjing was designed by the Hongwu Emperor (1328–1398) after he founded the Ming Dynasty (1368–1644) and established Nanjing as the capital in 1368. In order to consolidate his sovereignty and defend the city against coastal pirates, he adopted the suggestions of advisor Zhu Sheng to build a higher city wall, to collect grains and to postpone the coronation. The construction of the wall required the labor of 200,000 workers over twenty-one years to complete. Around 7 million cubic metres of earth were shifted. The City Wall of Nanjing was among the largest city walls ever constructed in China. The enclosed Nanjing City is about 55 square kilometers. Another great place to visit when you are in Nanjing.

Xuanwu Lake

Is a scenic area located near the zhonghua gate and one of the famous lakes in Nanjing. There are beautiful walk ways near the lake and also has a park known as Xuanwu park. If you are a person who likes to hike or climb, there’s a mountain nearby called Purple mountain and you can have beautiful views from above. The best time to visit the mountain is from mid-September to late November and March to May but you can go at any time to visit the park and enjoying the view of the lake to spend some good memories.

Qinhuai River

The Qinhuai River is a tributary of the Yangtze with a total length of 110 km. It flows through central Nanjing and is called "Nanjing's mother river". It is the "life blood" of the city. The Qinhuai River is divided into inner and outer rivers. The scenic belt along the Qinhuai River meets with the Confucius Temple at the center and the river serving as a bond. The belt, featuring attractions such as Zhanyuan Garden, the Confucius Temple, Egret Islet, China Gate as well as the sailing boats in the river and pavilions and towers on the riverbanks, is a blend of historic sites, gardens, barges, streets, pavilions, towers, folk culture and customs. This is surely a place to be if you really want to appreciate the beauty of nature.

Zhong hua gate

It’s one of the oldest walls that was built in ancient time during ming dynasty and this beautiful architecture is seen as a gathering of treasure.

Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall

This place is located at a mass grave at one of the sites of Nanjing Massacre. In order to exhibit this historical event, a combination of historical artifacts, architecture, sculptures, films and historical documents are used.
