Nantong

Located in the southeast of Jiangsu province, Nantong is known as the Pearl of the River and Sea which is also a major port on the Yangtze River near Shanghai that was historically known for its distinctive textiles and the birthplace of China's modern industry.

Suqian

Located in northern part of Jiangsu Province, Suqian is the world's center of biological evolution is also the center of human origin, known as the Earth "life sacred." Suqian is a city with a cultural history of 2,500 years. Suqian has a long history and every generation produces its man of ability.

Taizhou

Taizhou lies in the center of Jiangsu Province and Situated on the north bank of the Yangtze River Taizhou is one of those Chinese cities with a history that stretches very deep into the past. It was first settled some five thousand years ago and is home to both the only Great Wall in Southern China and a 1500-year-old monastery that founded a school of Buddhism.

Xuzhou

The beautiful Xuzhou is the largest city of northern Jiangsu with its long and rich history. It is the center of a rich coalfield, with mines in the immediate vicinity. Xuzhou, with its long and rich history, has been designated by the national government as one of China’s historical and cultural cities.

Yancheng

Yancheng has a history of 2,100 years since the first canton was founded here in Han Dynasty at year 119 BC. Charming Yancheng facing eastward to the Yellow Sea, has numerous famous historical sites and ruins. Yancheng means the city of salt, which implicates the developed salt industry in the past.

Yangzhou

A city located in the center of Jiangsu where the Yangtze River and the Beijing - Hangzhou Grand Canal meet. This is a small quiet city with a long history and was named as City of Gastronomy by UNESCO in 2019. In the early part of the 20th century, it remained relatively prosperous and remained a center of such traditional handicrafts as silk textiles, lacquer ware, carving, and embroidery. It was a regional market, especially for rice, and a center of food processing. The city won the UN Habitat Scroll of Honor award back in 2006. This place also has a lot of Chinese historical attractions like temples and museums.

Zhenjiang

The city was once the provincial capital of Jiangsu province. Zhenjiang a rich heritage of culture and art, the city is often associated with many of the fascinating folk mythologies in Chinese history. Zhenjiang has many well-known temples and other relics; important archaeological finds have also been made in the area. It is one of China’s busiest ports and has become a center of the lumber industry, the destination of huge log rafts floated down the Yangtze that are broken up there and shipped on to the consumers in the river delta and northern Jiangsu.
